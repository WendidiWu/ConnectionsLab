Introverted Fireworks

Interactive Installation | Arduino | Time-of-Flight Sensor

Introverted Fireworks is an interactive art installation that empowers the audience with the magic to control the fireworks.

The Introverted Fireworks will come to life as soon as the audience engages with it. Participants are invited to place their hand about 1 inch above
the sensor and move it up and down. The fireworks will illuminate in response to the height of the hand, creating a dynamic visual display.

My inspiration for this project came from the Time-of-Flight sensor itself. The first time I witnessed its workings, it felt like I was encountering a piece
of magic. This sense of wonder sparked my desire to create an experience that feels just as magical.

Fireworks came to mind as the subject for this installation because they embody the fleeting beauty we can never truly capture or preserve. They burst
into life, dazzling us for a brief moment before fading away, leaving only a memory. With this installation, I wanted to offer the audience not just the
chance to witness fireworks, but to possess the magic power to play with them, to hold that ephemeral beauty in their handsâ€”even if only for a moment.

However, this magic power comes with a cost. To preserve the beauty of the fireworks, the audience must remain engaged, continuously interacting with
the sensor. The moment they pull away, the magic vanishes, and the fireworks disappear, reminding us of the transient nature of beauty and the effort
required to sustain it.
